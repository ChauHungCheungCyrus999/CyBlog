My CityU CS Placement Experience

Today is the last day of my CityU CS Placement. It has been a really fun and enjoyable experience. My supervisor is a very nice lady, and I want to give a big shout-out to her.

I still remember last year, when I was trying to find information about the placement. I felt nervous and confused, but I could not find much useful information online. So here I am — I want to share the process and my experience so that you can understand it better.

## What is the CityU CS Placement?

The CS Placement is a graduation requirement for CityU CS students. That is also why you can see the 100% internship rate on the CityU CS website.

Some students may think that a 9-to-12-month placement is unnecessary. However, I truly believe that most students will end up loving this experience because it gives you real exposure to the working world before graduation.

## The Process of the CS Placement

I received the email from the CS department asking us to sign in to the PMS (Placement Management System) to register for placement. This was around Year 2 Semester A.

At that moment, my honest reaction was:

“HOLY F***?? I know nothing about coding. I only know some Python, some Java, and some frontend stuff. My GPA is so cooked too. Right now, you are asking me to find an internship!?”

That was when I realized I really had to start preparing for the placement.

Inside PMS, there are many different companies:

  • Around 50% are startups
  • Around 40% are big or mid-sized companies
  • Around 5–10% are banks

There are more than 100 job opportunities in total.

Not everyone will get a placement immediately, but if you work hard on preparation, I believe you will have a good chance.

Recently, I heard the system has changed. Previously, students could only pick five placements per round and get the contact information for those companies. Each round lasted about a month. But now, my friend says students can get the contact information for all placements in this year’s PMS.

### After getting the company contact information

Once you get the contact information of a company, the next step is the emailing stage:

  • Send your CV
  • You may also send your academic transcript
  • Then wait for the internship interview
### After the interview

After the interview, the company may contact you through:

  • Phone call
  • Zoom meeting
  • Face-to-face meeting

Then they will decide whether to give you an offer.

So always pay attention to your phone calls and emails.

If a company decides to offer you the placement, they may first contact you by phone or email, and then send the official offer through PMS.

In PMS, you usually have 5–7 days to decide whether to accept the offer.

Once you accept it, you cannot change it anymore.

And congratulations — you have found your internship!

## My Preparation for the Placement

At that time, I knew my GPA was not high enough to compete with everyone else, but I also knew I still had a chance.

There were mainly two kinds of students in PMS:

  1. Year 2 students who had never gone through the process before
  2. Year 3 students who had already tried once but failed to secure an internship

For me, I did not have both GPA and experience. So I thought the easiest way to compete was:

  • Do as many projects as I could
  • Perform well in interviews
### Projects

For projects, I strongly recommend building at least one full-stack website with:

  • Login
  • Registration
  • Basic frontend and backend features

With the help of modern LLM tools, this is much more achievable now.

I think everyone should have at least one project to showcase.

### Interviews

For interviews, companies usually do not expect you to have much working experience. What they care more about is:

  • Your knowledge
  • Your attitude
  • Your willingness to learn

Recently, many companies also like to ask about LLMs, so it is a good idea to prepare for that topic as well.

Also, always prepare some background information about the company before the interview.

## Is the Placement Stressful?

Honestly, it depends.

I know some people whose placements involved a lot of workload. One of my friends had to work overtime very often to finish tasks. But I think his case was more of a special one.

Most placements are actually quite chill. To be honest, companies generally do not expect you to know much at the beginning. As long as you are:

  • Nice to your coworkers
  • Willing to learn
  • Smart and responsible

it usually will not be too stressful.

## Is the Placement Worth It?

Yes — definitely worth it.

The first IT internship is always one of the hardest for students to get. Companies usually prefer candidates who already have some working experience, so this placement gives you a very valuable first step into the industry.

Even though the pay may not be a lot, the experience is extremely valuable.

For me, internship life was actually easier than studying courses.

My supervisor let me try many different things:

  • Frontend
  • Backend
  • Algorithms

Even if some of the tasks were basic, they still helped me explore different areas and understand more clearly what I want to do in the future.

I also improved my communication skills during the placement.

And of course, you also get a little taste of financial independence. You start buying your own clothes, trying new experiences, and enjoying the feeling of spending money you earned yourself.

That is also one of the nicest parts of the placement.

## Final Thoughts

If you are feeling nervous about the CityU CS Placement, that is completely normal. I felt the same way at the start.

But looking back now, I can say that this experience was genuinely meaningful and enjoyable. It gave me work experience, confidence, new skills, and a better idea of what I want for the future.

So if you are about to go through it, prepare well, stay calm, and give it your best shot.

You might end up enjoying it much more than you expect.
