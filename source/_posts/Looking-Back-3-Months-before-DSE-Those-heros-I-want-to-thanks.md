### Reason why I want to write this
<p>Why am I writing about the last 3 months? Because everything changed in that short time. These last 3 months were an unforgettable lesson for me. They taught me more than the seven years I spent in secondary school. I want to write this memory down so when I’m old, I can read it again and never forget. xD</p>
<hr />
### Background
<p>As I mentioned in my last blog, I was predicted only 12 marks in my last mock exam. I remember calling my mum during the mocks saying, “I want to give up… let’s just retake the DSE next year. It seems impossible.”</p>
<p>Back then, everyone thought I would never get into university (including myself). It wasn’t because people were mean; it was because I was doing really badly. I tried to study, but I just couldn’t focus. I was stuck in a loop: Trying to study -&gt; get a bad grade -&gt; feel sad -&gt; try again -&gt; get another bad grade. (Hahaha, I’m not lying. I lost count of how many times I failed).</p>
<p>In Jan 2023, I was very frustrated with my Chinese writing. Every time I wrote, I got a low grade. I tried again and again, but nothing changed. Even after my teacher explained my mistakes, I still didn’t “get” it. I knew I’d fail the DSE if I didn’t solve this. I could feel my teacher losing patience, which was totally understandable, honestly.</p>
<p>“What should I do right now?” I felt hopeless, but I had to find a way. I remembered a very good teacher, Miss Zhu, who used to teach me. She once said she’d give us advice if we asked. So, I went to the staff room, used the mic and said, “Excuse me, I’m looking for Miss Zhu.” I handed her a piece of writing and asked for help. She said yes and told me to collect it the next day.</p>
<p>When we met in the library, she was blunt: “Your writing will never pass the DSE if you keep doing this.” She had written so many comments on my paper and explained them one by one. Honestly, I’ve forgotten some of the specific skills now xD, but at the time, I was still doing badly and felt totally cooked.</p>
<hr />
### The twist - Miss Zhu
<p>With only 3 months left before the DSE, the school arranged for us to study together in the big hall. Of course, I was there. xD I was so frustrated because I couldn’t even break a score of 50. I was just laying my head on one of those small wooden exam desks when a hand touched my shoulder.</p>
<p>It was Miss Zhu. She called me over to talk about my writing again. She was the one who came to me right when I was about to give up. In that moment, I felt a huge shift: “I can’t let her down, because she actually thinks I still have a chance.” It’s a crazy feeling when everyone (including you) has given up, but one person still has hope. How can you give up then?</p>
<p>I listened to her. She told me she had helped students go from a Level 1 to a Level 3 before, and she was sure I could do it too. “Alright, let’s follow everything she says.” She taught me that every writing has a pattern. Just follow the pattern, and it should be fine.</p>
<p>After that, I started spamming writing exercises. I don’t know how many I did, but it was a lot. My grades—and my hope—started going up. Once I hit a Level 3 or 4 in writing, I moved on to other subjects. I spent my days in the library, sitting at the computer desks, printing out exercises and doing them over and over.</p>

### Lesson learnt
<p>I experience and learn a lot things from these 3 months. a lot a lot. The main lesson I learnt is what attitude should I have when I face some troubles. Never give up urself even noone believe on you. Never. Ever. Please. If you really want a thing, u should go for it even u know u have a huge chance to lost it. Why dont give it a try? You will be suprise how strong ur ability really is. Also find the one who believe on you and dont blame on others. Look at what problem u have first, then try to fix it on urself.</p>
