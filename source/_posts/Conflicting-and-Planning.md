# Why I'm Writing This Post

It's been a while since my last blog post, and I feel it's time to reflect on my journey and plan for the future.

After my exams and internship ended, I took some time to relax and enjoy myself. However, I soon started to feel empty and lost, unsure of what to do next. My goals felt unclear, and daily leisure wasn't helping me find direction. So, I've decided to take a step back, assess where I am, and figure out my next steps, much like I did last year. I've come to the CityU library to gather my thoughts.

# What I Want to Achieve

Honestly, my goal is both achievable and realistic: I want to find a meaningful job that contributes to society and allows me to earn enough to support a future family. I envision a simple, normal life with a loving wife by my side and a little kid sleeping on my chest. That’s all I want.

Every time I see my uncle and auntie smile and laugh at each other's jokes, I'm reminded of how beautiful that is. I want that for myself. My plan is to find a job with a career path that could lead to a salary of over 30k in the future. I also need to build a stronger body to protect my future family and find someone who truly loves me.

# Where I Am Now

I've made a lot of progress compared to the last two years. I remember searching "How to unfuck my life" on YouTube, where creators offered basic, realistic advice to help people get started. I'm grateful that I've moved past that stage and am in a much better place this year. My skin is clearer, I'm in better shape, and I feel more confident, social, and mature. I used to struggle with conversations, but I'm glad I've overcome that. It took time, but it was worth it. If you need advice on this, let me know.

With only one year of my student experience left, I know I need to make it count.

Whenever I chat with my friend Hei, I see the passion he has for IT, and I can't quite relate. For me, my CS degree and coding skills feel more like a backup plan—a chance to get to know myself better before settling into a 9-to-6 job.

I've also been volunteering with various disciplined services, and I've found the police department most interesting. Although I know many people in Hong Kong have negative views of the police, every officer I've met has been kind and dedicated to their duty. I want to be a part of that and contribute to society in a meaningful way. I know it's a high-pressure job and difficult to get into, but I'm genuinely interested.

# My Short-Term Goals

In terms of fitness, I'm shifting my focus from building size to improving my running ability and overall fitness.

I'm also preparing to go on exchange to Canada on July 17th, which is just next week. I need to make sure everything is in order for my arrival. I hope to meet new people and successfully complete my two courses there.

After the exchange, I'll have to tackle my final year project—a basketball VR project. I'm not sure if it will work out, but I want to build the predictive model before I leave for exchange.

Next year, I plan to apply for more volunteer opportunities with the disciplined services to gain more experience and learn more about the job I'm so interested in.

I also need to practice my English speaking skills and train my brain to think in English. It's all about increasing my strengths and minimizing my weaknesses to prepare for future job applications.

# Summary

That's all for now. I don't expect many people to read this, but it's a true reflection of my thoughts and feelings. It's 9:18 PM on July 4th, and I'm still here in the library, writing this. Let's see what the future holds. I hope everyone who reads this has a great day! - Cy
