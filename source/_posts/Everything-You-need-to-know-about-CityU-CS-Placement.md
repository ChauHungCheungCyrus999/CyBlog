# My CityU CS Placement Experience
<p>Today is the last day of my CityU CS Placement. It has been a really fun and enjoyable experience. My supervisor is a very nice lady, and I want to give a big shout-out to her.</p>
<p>I still remember last year, when I was trying to find information about the placement. I felt nervous and confused, but I could not find much useful information online. So here I am — I want to share the process and my experience so that you can understand it better.</p>
## What is the CityU CS Placement?
<p>The CS Placement is a graduation requirement for CityU CS students. That is also why you can see the <strong>100% internship rate</strong> on the CityU CS website.</p>
<p>Some students may think that a <strong>9-to-12-month placement</strong> is unnecessary. However, I truly believe that most students will end up loving this experience because it gives you real exposure to the working world before graduation.</p>
## The Process of the CS Placement
<p>I received the email from the CS department asking us to sign in to the <strong>PMS (Placement Management System)</strong> to register for placement. This was around <strong>Year 2 Semester A</strong>.</p>
<p>At that moment, my honest reaction was:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>“HOLY F***?? I know nothing about coding. I only know some Python, some Java, and some frontend stuff. My GPA is so cooked too. Right now, you are asking me to find an internship!?”</p>
</blockquote>
<p>That was when I realized I really had to start preparing for the placement.</p>
<p>Inside PMS, there are many different companies:</p>
<ul>
<li>Around <strong>50% are startups</strong></li>
<li>Around <strong>40% are big or mid-sized companies</strong></li>
<li>Around <strong>5–10% are banks</strong></li>
</ul>
<p>There are <strong>more than 100 job opportunities</strong> in total.</p>
<p>Not everyone will get a placement immediately, but if you work hard on preparation, I believe you will have a good chance.</p>
<p>Recently, I heard the system has changed. Previously, students could only pick <strong>five placements per round</strong> and get the contact information for those companies. Each round lasted about a month. But now, my friend says students can get the contact information for <strong>all placements</strong> in this year’s PMS.</p>
### After getting the company contact information
<p>Once you get the contact information of a company, the next step is the <strong>emailing stage</strong>:</p>
<ul>
<li>Send your <strong>CV</strong></li>
<li>You may also send your <strong>academic transcript</strong></li>
<li>Then wait for the <strong>internship interview</strong></li>
</ul>
### After the interview
<p>After the interview, the company may contact you through:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Phone call</strong></li>
<li><strong>Zoom meeting</strong></li>
<li><strong>Face-to-face meeting</strong></li>
</ul>
<p>Then they will decide whether to give you an offer.</p>
<p>So always pay attention to your phone calls and emails.</p>
<p>If a company decides to offer you the placement, they may first contact you by phone or email, and then send the official offer through <strong>PMS</strong>.</p>
<p>In PMS, you usually have <strong>5–7 days</strong> to decide whether to accept the offer.</p>
<p>Once you accept it, <strong>you cannot change it anymore</strong>.</p>
<p>And congratulations — you have found your internship!</p>
## My Preparation for the Placement
<p>At that time, I knew my GPA was not high enough to compete with everyone else, but I also knew I still had a chance.</p>
<p>There were mainly <strong>two kinds of students</strong> in PMS:</p>
<ol>
<li><strong>Year 2 students</strong> who had never gone through the process before</li>
<li><strong>Year 3 students</strong> who had already tried once but failed to secure an internship</li>
</ol>
<p>For me, I did not have both GPA and experience. So I thought the easiest way to compete was:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Do as many projects as I could</strong></li>
<li><strong>Perform well in interviews</strong></li>
</ul>
### Projects
<p>For projects, I strongly recommend building at least <strong>one full-stack website</strong> with:</p>
<ul>
<li>Login</li>
<li>Registration</li>
<li>Basic frontend and backend features</li>
</ul>
<p>With the help of modern LLM tools, this is much more achievable now.</p>
<p>I think everyone should have <strong>at least one project to showcase</strong>.</p>
### Interviews
<p>For interviews, companies usually do <strong>not expect you to have much working experience</strong>. What they care more about is:</p>
<ul>
<li>Your <strong>knowledge</strong></li>
<li>Your <strong>attitude</strong></li>
<li>Your <strong>willingness to learn</strong></li>
</ul>
<p>Recently, many companies also like to ask about <strong>LLMs</strong>, so it is a good idea to prepare for that topic as well.</p>
<p>Also, always prepare some background information about the company before the interview.</p>
## Is the Placement Stressful?
<p><strong>Honestly, it depends.</strong></p>
<p>I know some people whose placements involved a lot of workload. One of my friends had to work overtime very often to finish tasks. But I think his case was more of a special one.</p>
<p>Most placements are actually quite chill. To be honest, companies generally do not expect you to know much at the beginning. As long as you are:</p>
<ul>
<li>Nice to your coworkers</li>
<li>Willing to learn</li>
<li>Smart and responsible</li>
</ul>
<p>it usually will not be too stressful.</p>
## Is the Placement Worth It?
<p><strong>Yes — definitely worth it.</strong></p>
<p>The first IT internship is always one of the hardest for students to get. Companies usually prefer candidates who already have some working experience, so this placement gives you a very valuable first step into the industry.</p>
<p>Even though the pay may not be a lot, the experience is extremely valuable.</p>
<p>For me, internship life was actually easier than studying courses.</p>
<p>My supervisor let me try many different things:</p>
<ul>
<li>Frontend</li>
<li>Backend</li>
<li>Algorithms</li>
</ul>
<p>Even if some of the tasks were basic, they still helped me explore different areas and understand more clearly what I want to do in the future.</p>
<p>I also improved my <strong>communication skills</strong> during the placement.</p>
<p>And of course, you also get a little taste of <strong>financial independence</strong>. You start buying your own clothes, trying new experiences, and enjoying the feeling of spending money you earned yourself.</p>
<p>That is also one of the nicest parts of the placement.</p>
## Final Thoughts
<p>If you are feeling nervous about the CityU CS Placement, that is completely normal. I felt the same way at the start.</p>
<p>But looking back now, I can say that this experience was genuinely meaningful and enjoyable. It gave me work experience, confidence, new skills, and a better idea of what I want for the future.</p>
<p>So if you are about to go through it, prepare well, stay calm, and give it your best shot.</p>
<p>You might end up enjoying it much more than you expect.</p>
